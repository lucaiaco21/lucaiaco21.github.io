The Challenge

Main Objective of the Project

The main objective was to help a coffee shop in Madrid to predict the weekly sales in order to reduce food waste and optimize the buying process. To predict the sales serveral aspects were taking into consideration such as weather forecast, number of tourist expected in a close by museum and lag of last 14 days. The model output two forecast one for total sales and one for total units sold by product category.

In order to increase the sales and differenciate from competition, an analysis of online reviews of the coffee shop and nearby competitors was done in order to have an overview of what customer loved most about competitions and what are the strenghts and issues of the coffee shop analyzed

Through Streamlit an interface was developed so that owner of the coffee shop could easily access the analysis of reviews and do by himself the prediction only by uploading invoices of the last two weeks

The Approach

Sales Forecasting Model

  • 01 Data Preprocessing & Feature Engineering A total of 900.000 invoices (L3 Years) were used to train the data. After the EDA and the grouping of the variables per product category, several varibales were added such as the temperature of that day, if it was raining and how much, the total number of visitors of the nearby museum and a 14 day variable lag
  • 02 Training and Model Choice - Why Prophet?

    To choose the best model, first were tried the mos common model used for Time Series analysis such as ARIMA/SARIMA that were dismissed for the difficulties to add exogenous variables while the ML classic model liek Linear Regression and XGBoost were used as baseline. Among all the others models tried, like Naive or LSTM, the one that best suited the problem was Prophet.

    Prophet, a model developed by META, was choose for several caharcteristics such as the fact that is very good to detect weekly and annual seasonality and trends. There is also the possibility to add a list of days that have a bigger impact on the sales, perfect for our case where we could add several holidays that over the years had huge impact oon the sales. To the model can also be add easily some exogenous variables that can have an additive or multuplicative effect.

  • 03 Web Scraping & Sentence Tokenizer For the reviews analysis, first were scraped almost 30k of reviews from Google Maps and Trip Advisors of the main competitors. Then, after a cleaning, a sentence tokenizer was used in order to detect multiple sentiment inside the single reviews. So a review like “Matcha was very good and we loved the carrot cake but we waited too much time to be served” was splitted into two sentences with opposite sentiment.
  • 04 Keyword Extract & Semantic Normalization

    Then were extracted up to 10 keywords per review with YAKE (up to 3‑word n‑grams), using only statistical signals, which works well on noisy review text and with a all‑MiniLM‑L6‑v2 Sentence Transformers was computed cosine similarity between keywords to cluster semantically equivalent terms and assign a canonical label.

    The final output here are insights, divided by category and sentiment, to help the owner take actions based on the products loved most about competitors menu, if there was any issue with its price, type of location and decoration preferred etc....

  • 05 Interface Creation w/ Streamlit Both the prediction model and the review analysis are displayed in a Streamlit Interface. fully deployed with Python code, to help the owner easily access those information.
