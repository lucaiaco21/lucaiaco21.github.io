Overview

This project explores 20 years of global refugee data — combining UNHCR displacement records, ACLED conflict events, and World Bank GDP figures to understand what drives forced migration and where people actually go when they go away from their country.

001 GDP & Displacement Animated Bubble Chart
GDP vs Refugees Over Time

Each bubble is a country — size reflects refugee numbers, vertical position shows GDP. Press play to watch 20 years of displacement unfold. The pattern is clear: economic collapse and conflict go hand in hand with forced migration. Low GDP countries dominate the top of the refugee rankings, consistently, year after year giving the insights that is not only war but also poverty to push people to migrate.

Key finding
Low GDP = High displacement

003 Origin → Destination Interactive Treemap
Where Refugees Actually Go

Each rectangle is a country pair — origin and asylum. Size shows refugee volume. Click any country to drill down and see exactly which nations absorb its refugees. The finding is counterintuitive: refugees don't go to wealthy nations — they go to nearby ones. Pakistan, Iran, Uganda and Bangladesh absorb more than Germany or the US combined but at the same time are the richest country to complain more about immigration. Funny isn't it?

Key finding
